About Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

What is AML?

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) refers to laws, regulations, and procedures intended to prevent criminals from disguising illegally obtained funds as legitimate income.

Why is AML Important?

Money laundering enables organized crime, corruption, and terrorism. AML efforts help ensure financial systems remain secure and trustworthy.

Global Impact of AML Efforts

AML initiatives require global cooperation, as criminals often move money across borders. The Financial Action Task Force (FATF) sets international standards for AML compliance.
